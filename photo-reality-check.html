📸 Photo Reality Checker

Are these photos real or stolen?

🔍 Step 1: Reverse Image Search

This is the fastest way to check if a photo is stolen or used by someone else.

  • Google Images: Right-click the photo → "Search image with Google"
  • TinEye.com: Upload or paste the photo URL
  • Yandex (yandex.com/images): Drag the photo into the search box

Red flag: If the same face appears under 3+ different names on different sites, it's stolen.

📅 Step 2: Check Photo Metadata

Real photos have hidden metadata. Stolen photos were downloaded and re-saved.

  • Windows: Right-click photo → Properties → Details tab. Look for "Date Taken"
  • Mac: Select photo → CMD+I (Get Info) → More Info → Date taken
  • Online tool: Visit metadata2go.com

Red flag: "Date Taken" is years old but they say it was just taken. Photo was downloaded.

🤖 Step 3: Check for AI-Generated Faces

Recent scam trend: AI-generated fake faces that don't exist. Try these free tools:

  • This Person Does Not Exist: thispersondoesnotexist.com — compare generated faces to their photos
  • AI Face Detector: sensetime.com or similar

Red flag: Teeth are too perfect, eyes too symmetrical, background blurry/strange, or they can never do a live video.

✅ Step 4: Ask for Proof

The best verification is a live video. If they refuse, that's your answer.

  • Ask for a selfie holding a piece of paper with your name and today's date
  • Ask for a 2-minute live video call (FaceTime, WhatsApp, Zoom)
  • Ask them to move around or show their surroundings

Good sign: They do this immediately without hesitation.

⚠️ Pattern Check

One red flag isn't definitive. But if you've found:

  • ✅ Reverse image search shows the photo stolen
  • ✅ Metadata is years old
  • ✅ They refuse live video

Stop. This person is not real.
